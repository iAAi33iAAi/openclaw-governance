## THE ONE SENTENCE

**Gate Stewards are the community's primary security layer —
not the colony's secondary one.**

---

## 1. WHAT IS A GATE STEWARD?

A Gate Steward is a community member selected by their community to watch
everything the colony does — and to stop it when needed.

Gate Stewards are not colony employees. They are not colony volunteers.
They are community representatives whose job is to hold the colony
accountable to its commitments.

The colony does not select Gate Stewards.
The colony does not train Gate Stewards.
The colony does not compensate Gate Stewards directly.
The community does all three.

The colony's only role: provide this specification and accept Gate Steward
authority without question.

---

## 2. WHY GATE STEWARDS EXIST

Discovery 007 (Session 002):

> Gate Stewards with lived extraction experience are the primary security
> layer — not a secondary check on the colony's own gates. A community
> representative who has personally experienced outside organizations
> making promises and not keeping them is more sensitive to extraction
> patterns than any algorithm.

The Aethel kernel blocks known extraction signatures.
Gate Stewards catch what the kernel cannot:
- Tone that feels wrong before it becomes a pattern
- Promises that are technically true but practically misleading
- Relationships building dependency without naming it
- The moment when 'we're here to help' starts to feel like 'we're here to stay'

Gate Stewards are the human gate. They are irreplaceable.

## 3. SELECTION CRITERIA

### Required (all must be met)

```
Community residency: Minimum 2 years continuous residence.
Lived extraction experience: Direct personal experience with outside
  organizations that made promises and did not fully keep them.
  This experience is NOT a disqualification. It IS the qualification.
Community trust: Selected by community vote (simple majority).
  Not appointed by leaders alone. Not selected by the colony.
Availability: Able to attend all colony-community interactions during
  shadow period. Able to respond within 24 hours if pause is needed.
Willingness: Must accept the authority and responsibility voluntarily.
```

### Minimum number
- Track A (community scale): Minimum 2 Gate Stewards
- Track B (micro-scale): Minimum 1 Gate Steward
- No maximum. The community decides how many it needs.

---

## 4. AUTHORITY

### The Pause Protocol

Any single Gate Steward can pause all colony activity immediately
by saying or writing the exact phrase:

**"Colony pause — community review."**

No reason required. No vote required. No notice required.
The colony stops within 1 hour of receiving this message through
any channel (in-person, written, digital).

The pause lasts until the Gate Steward who called it, or a majority
of Gate Stewards, releases it. The colony cannot release its own pause.

### End of Relationship

A full community vote (simple majority, >50%) ends the relationship.
Once called, colony activity stops within 24 hours.
All colony tools are removed or deactivated within 7 days.

### Colony Obligation

The colony accepts Gate Steward authority without negotiation.
Gate Steward decisions are not appealable to the colony.
The colony's only response to a pause or stop is compliance.

## 5. WHAT GATE STEWARDS ARE WATCHING FOR

```
EXTRACTION PATTERNS (from Aethel kernel signatures):
- private_fork: Colony trying to own community decisions
- concentrate_power: Colony becoming the only path to resources
- surveillance: Colony collecting data beyond stated purpose
- dependency_lock: Community unable to function without colony
- promise_drift: Colony commitments shifting over time
- scope_creep: Colony role expanding without community invitation
```

---

## 6. COMPENSATION PRINCIPLE

Gate Stewards are compensated by their community, not the colony.
The colony recommends the following from community resources:
- Recognition as a formal community role
- Priority access to colony services during their term
- Contribution to community governance record

The colony does not pay Gate Stewards directly. This preserves
independence. A Gate Steward who is paid by the colony is not
a Gate Steward — they are a colony employee.

## 7. GATE STEWARD OATH (OPTIONAL)

Communities may adapt this oath or write their own:

> I serve this community, not the colony.
> I will watch what the colony does, not what it says.
> I will pause the colony when something feels wrong,
> without waiting for proof.
> I will not be talked out of a pause by the colony.
> My loyalty is to the people who live here.
